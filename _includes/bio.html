About Me

I'm Miguel, a second-year PhD student at the Robotics, Computer Vision and Artificial Intelligence lab (RoPeRT) at the University of Zaragoza (Unizar). I'm researching Bayesian learning and Bayesian optimization approaches to assistive technologies, under the supervision of Prof. Ruben Martinez-Cantin. My focus is on multimodal and human-feedback-based problems, and how to optimize and approach interactions between humans and AI models. I have dipped my toes into music, generative models, event cameras, and biosignals, and I hope to use my current and future knowledge to help people.

I'm also interested in arts, crafts and how to tangle them with my computer scientist side. I will always complain about my thesis being time-consuming but happily spend hours coding add-ons for a Dungeons and Dragons campaign. My colleagues also like to come to me for tarot readings.
